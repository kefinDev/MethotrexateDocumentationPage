Methotrexate Product Monograph
Indications Mechanism of Action Dosing and Administration Adverse Effects Drug Interactions Contraindications Resources
Indications

Disease modifying antirheumatic drug (DMARD) for treatment of:

  • Rheumatoid Arthritis
  • Psoriatic arthritis

Chemotherapy for various forms of cancer including:

  • Breast cancer
  • Leukemia
  • Gastric cancer
  • Bladder cancer
  • Cancer of bone
Mechanism of Action

Inhibits dihydrofolate reductase thereby preventing DNA syntehsis, repair, and cell replication. Dihydrofolate reductase converts folic acid to tetrahydrofolate, which is required for DNA synthesis. Actively replicating cells such as cancer cells, bone marrow, and intestinal cells are more sensitive to this effect.

Cancer Various weight- and BSA-based dosing regimens Dependent on type of cancer

Abbreviations

PO: by mouth; SC: subcutaneous; IM: intramuscular; IV: intravenous; Q12H: every 12 hours; BSA: Body Surface Area.
Adverse Effects

Most Common Adverse Reactions

  • Gastrointestinal: Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, ulcerative stomatitis
  • Hepatic: elevated liver enzymes

Less Common Adverse Reactions

  • Central nervous system: headache, dizziness
  • Other: leukopenia, alopecia
Drug Interactions

NSAIDs: reduce renal elimination of methotrexate.

Unpredictable severe reactions including death

Alcohol: may enhance hepatotoxic effect of methotrexate

Limit alcohol consumption

Ciprofloxacin: may increase serum concentration of methotrexate

Proton pump inhibitrs: may increase serum concentration of methotrexate

Tacrolimus/Cyclosporine: may enhance toxic immunosuppresant effects of both drugs

Contraindications

Pregnancy: may cause fetal death, abortion, or birth defects.

Breastfeeding: potential for serious adverse reactions in breastfed infants.

Alcoholic liver disease or other chronic liver disease.

Immunodeficiency syndromes

Blood dyscrasias including leukopenia, thrombocytopenia, significant anemia, and bone marrow hypoplasia
